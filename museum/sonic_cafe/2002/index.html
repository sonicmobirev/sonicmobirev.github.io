Sonic Cafe (2002)

2002 is the year where Sonic Cafe received the biggest number of Sonic games.

Since September 24th, a selection of Sonic Team designed papercrafts were available for purchase at Lawson locations in Japan using their i-print service. Some designs were exclusive to this service, while others were available from the CHAO Laboratory for at-home printing.

A Sonic themed golf game. In this game, the player plays half a round (nine holes) of golf as Sonic. New rounds could be downloaded from Sonic Cafe servers on the main menu by pressing [0].

Sonic Fishing

A Sonic the Hedgehog themed fishing game. The player chooses a lure based on Sonic, Tails or Eggman and tries to catch as many fish as possible within a time limit.

An enhanced port was released outside of Japan. It plays identically to this game, but has been graphically overhauled.

Sonic Billiards

A Sonic themed billiards simulation game. In this game, the player plays as Sonic in a nine-ball game of billiards.

Nakayoshi Chao! (なかよしチャオ！)

A virtual pet game featuring the Chao of the Sonic series. Players would be able to pet the Chao, talk to it, play games such as Rock Paper Scissors, and download wallpapers for the game.

Sonic Bowling

A Sonic themed bowling simulation game. In this game, Sonic, Tails, Knuckles and Shadow compete at bowling. Up to four players can play, with computers as the other three players if requested.

The Southeast Asian version for J2ME has been dumped, but not the original Japanese version or the also released overseas i-mode version from Zenops. The non-Japanese versions were released later and feature updated graphics.

Sonic Racing Kart (ソニックレーシング KART)

A Sonic the Hedgehog kart racing game. Gameplay is viewed from a top-down, third person perspective. Two different variations of this view are able to be chosen: Car Rotation and Course Rotation.

The J2ME port of this game for South America has been dumped, though with some graphical upgrades. It's also known that there was a Southeast Asian J2ME release that is lost.

Sonic Golf C-Mode Cup (ソニックゴルフ Cmodeカップ)

A version of Sonic Golf released for an event held by Coca-Cola Japan. Each in-game point was worth 1 Yen, and could be used to purchase beverages from C-mode Coca-Cola vending machines.

The first event ran from September 9th to October 8th, and the second from November 25th to December 25th. The game was made unavailable once the events concluded.
