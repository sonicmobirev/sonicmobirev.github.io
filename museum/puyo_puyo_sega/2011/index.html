GAME LIST

Puyo Puyo! Sega (2011)

In 2011, Puyo Puyo! Sega released the last Sonic game for i-mode phones and the first Sonic games for Android (Sonic Jump and Sonic Advance).


Sonic Golf 3D (ソニックゴルフ3D)

A Sonic themed golf game. This game is a 3D remastered version of Sonic Golf DX released the previous year. This version has the ability to be displayed in stereoscopic 3D.

In this game, the player takes control of Sonic and his friends as they play golf. The player goes through a nine-hole course. Good shots earn Rings which can be used to purchase additional clubs, balls, accessories, special shots, and playable characters. In addition to the normal game, there is a training mode and download shop for swapping out courses. The game also has an online ranking; when earning high scores, the player can upload them onto a national scores table.

Date 2011
Platforms i-mode 90X
Preserved Yes (can be found in Keitai World Launcher)

Sonic Jump (2011)

A Sonic jumping platformer game. Sonic jumps up on platforms to reach the top of the stage. This version of Sonic Jump is a remake of the AirPlay version of Sonic Jump (also known as Sonic Jump 2 in certain regions).

The game features a grand total of 21 stages indicated by zone names and act numbers. Each zone has 3 acts. Additionally, these versions introduce Emerald Shards. If you clear a stage with at 50 rings, you will earn a shard. Collect all Emerald Shards in the game to unlock the brand-new Bonus Zone and end the story.

A Chinese version of the Android port was also released, but is currently lost.

Sonic Advance (ソニックアドバンス)

A port of the Game Boy Advance game Sonic Advance to Android devices.

The game itself is nearly identical to the Game Boy Advance version. The graphics are upscaled from their original resolution.

The Chinese version was released by a Chinese mobile service named eGame. The release date of this version is unkown, but it is speculated to be released in 2011 or 2012, judging at some files in the game assets.

Tegaki Sonic Jump (そにっくじゃんぷ)

A Sonic jumping platformer game. Sonic jumps up on platforms to reach as high as possible.

This game is the second installment in the hand-drawn Sonic "Sonic the Sketchhog" series. Like in Sonic Freehand, the more you advance in the level, the harder the difficulty will become.

This game was released exclusively in Japan as a free online Flash game for mobile devices on the Puyo Puyo! Sega service. Information about the release date is currently unknown.
